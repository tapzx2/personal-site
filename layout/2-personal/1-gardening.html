Gardening

I love gardens! Both growing and visiting them. From 2015 - 2021 my focus was our Phoenix backyard garden. Right now I don't get to get my hands dirty as much as I would like. Instead, I enjoy a visiting gardens and learning about plants with iNaturalist.

Phoenix 2015 - 2021

Gardening in Phoenix was a bit of a challenge. It freezes enough to be dangerous to tropicals, it gets really hot, and there's not much water. People from Iraq are in the Phoenix gardening groups because we have a similar climate.

My inspiration came from visiting friends with productive yards and the dream boat that is Monty Don. The show Big Dreams Small Spaces was a huge catalyst. Casually strolling your property and enjoying it's produce is a beautiful way to live. Particularly if you garden in an efficient way! Buzz words include: food forest, permaculture, organic, and low water.

Some strengths of the Phoenix area is that it was an agricultural center. It has good dirt. There are people who've been really really successful in Phoenix who provide plenty of free advice like Jake Mace, Shamus O'Leary, and Greg Peterson.

Some opportunities are Chip Drop (low competition) and free stuff from neighbors friends, and big sales at nurseries.

Threats: disgruntled neighbors, city regulations, pests, and severe heat / cold.

The main tenants of the yard came to be:

  • make shade
  • choose abuse tolerant, edible plants
  • mulch A LOT
  • add sulfur
  • buy cheap

Through trial, error, some free friend labor, and a court case this is the before and after.
