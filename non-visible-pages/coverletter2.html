Do you have experience with entitlements, real estate development, cities, etc?

Ryan Johnson informed me these were the things you were looking for. To be honest, I had never heard of the word entailments before. With an afternoon of research I found that I do have experience with parts of the entitlement process.

As I've come to understand entitlement covers a broad to specific hierarchy, going from:

  • Federal, State, and Regional Agency Requirements
  • Comprehensive Plan / General Plan
  • Zoning
  • Land Development Regulations
  • Permits

I have experience with reading comprehensive and general plans, it is something we did often in the city planning intensive at UC Berkeley. When deciding where move to I also reviewed Phoenix's general plan to make sure it was a reasonably well run city.

I'm familiar with reading zoning permissions and land use regulations. More recently, it is something I've had to research when assessing the risk exposure of our (relatively) unique business model. I'm most familiar with the spatial layers that cities provide that define their zoning requirement.

I have no experience with land development regulations or permits.

I'm very confident with acquiring and using spatial information. I'm comfortable interacting with bureaucratic organizations and finding information.

I understand that the entitlement process is not always a straight forward, tiered system. Interdependent systems, where each level can prevent a project and not all reviewing agencies share the same perspective on approval criteria, are something that I have dealt with a lot in computer programing. Admittedly, the documentation is better in the computer development field, but I'm confident I could fit the puzzle pieces together of an interdependent entitlement system.

Did you really just put being a home owner as real estate experience?

Typical US home ownership comes with a lot of emotional baggage. We committed ourselves to avoiding as much of it as possible. Buying land and property is a serious investment of capital and time. When selecting a home to buy we did a lot of finical and location due diligence, approaching its a real estate investment instead of as an emotional purchase.

We did site suitability analyses for regions with these layers:

  • Where we be willing to live
  • Proximity to transit
  • Proximity to grocery stores
  • Average rental income
  • Census income data
  • Violent crime data
  • Number of mature trees
  • Proximity to popular commercial districts
  • Nearby commercial property vacancy rates
  • Energy efficiency (solar panels)

It was a lot of work and so far we have been rewarded for it. Expenses are low, we've never had trouble getting housemates, and our neighborhood has appreciated significantly since we bought our home. I recently saw via Instagram that a member of your staff selected this neighborhood as a good location as well!
